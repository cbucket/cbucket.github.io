Notes about DND.

27/04/15

Got a great new link for DND resources.  Figured out that the maps used in DND are simply a 20*20*20 grid of bytes.
Each byte represents wall,doors,secret walls etc + what is found there (or not).

The mapping for what the number mean is described at the site but the following table shows the mapping.

Low Nibble

(1) 0001 - Wall (Ver)
(2) 0010 - Door (Ver)
(3) 0011 - Secret Door (Ver)
(4) 0100 - Wall (Hor)
(8) 1000 - Door (Hor)
(12)1100 - Secret Door (Hor)

High Nibble

    0000 - nothing
(1) 0001 - DN   
(2) 0010 - UP
(3) 0011 - SP  = 3  (SP = spiral staircase. i.e. UP + DOWN)
(4) 0100 - EXC
(5) 0101 - PIT
(6) 0110 - TPT
(7) 0111 - FNT
(8) 1000 - ALT
(9) 1001 - DGN
(10) 1010 - ORB = 10 = A hex
(11) 1011 - MIR (B)
(12) 1100 - ELV (C)
(13) 1101 - $   (D)
(14) 1110 - TRV (E)
(15) 1111 - GNI (F)

I used linux hexdump to decode the cavern.bin file found in the 1984 version of DND.

It was (fairly) easy to figure out the formatting by inspecting the values.  The first 20
values are the top line of the map. Two of the walls are implied from the adjacent cells therefore
only the right and bottom walls (it might be the other 2 though) are described in the byte value.
